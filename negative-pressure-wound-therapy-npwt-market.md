<p><p>Negative Pressure Wound Therapy (NPWT) is a specialized medical treatment designed to promote healing in acute and chronic wounds by applying negative pressure to the wound site. This therapy involves the use of a sealed wound dressing connected to a vacuum pump, which helps to remove excess fluid, reduce bacterial contamination, and stimulate tissue perfusion. NPWT is increasingly utilized for various wound types, including surgical wounds, diabetic ulcers, and pressure ulcers.</p><p>The NPWT market is witnessing significant growth, driven by a rising prevalence of chronic wounds and an increasing aging population. Key trends include the development of advanced NPWT devices that are more portable and user-friendly, enhancing patient comfort and compliance. Additionally, the integration of smart technology in NPWT systems is enabling better monitoring and management of wound healing processes. Moreover, the expansion of healthcare infrastructure in emerging markets is contributing to the market's growth.
